Choosing who teaches your child the Quran is one of the most important decisions a parent will make. A good teacher builds a lifelong love for the Quran; the wrong fit can put a child off learning altogether. Here's what to actually look for.

## Why the Right Teacher Matters More Than the Right App

Parents often focus on platforms, pricing, or scheduling apps — but the person on the other side of the screen is what determines whether a child stays engaged, corrects their Tajweed properly, and looks forward to class each week. Outline what changes when the teacher is a good match: patience, consistency, trust.

## Key Qualifications to Look For

### Ijazah and Formal Certification

Explain what an Ijazah is, why it matters for Tajweed and Hifz instruction specifically, and how parents can politely ask a teacher or academy to confirm this.

### Experience Teaching Children Specifically

Being a strong reciter doesn't automatically mean someone can teach a 7-year-old. Cover what to ask about: experience with young learners, patience with mistakes, ability to keep a child's attention for a 30–45 minute class.

### Fluency in a Shared Language

Cover why clear communication (English or the family's home language) matters for explaining rules, correcting mistakes, and keeping the child comfortable asking questions.

## Questions to Ask Before Committing

- How do you handle a child who is shy or loses focus mid-class?
- What does a typical first month look like?
- How do you track and report progress to parents?
- What happens if a class needs to be rescheduled?
- Can we sit in on a free trial class first?

## Red Flags to Watch For

Cover warning signs: no trial class offered, no verifiable credentials, pressure to commit to long-term payment upfront, unwillingness to let a parent observe a class, or a teaching style that relies purely on repetition without correction.

## Why a Free Trial Class Is Non-Negotiable

Explain how a trial class lets parents see the teacher's patience, teaching style, and rapport with the child in action — the single best signal before committing to a schedule.

## Final Thoughts

Wrap up with a short summary: qualifications matter, but so does fit — and the only way to know if a teacher is right for your child is to watch a real class together.
